## 1. “Stress shows up as: Air / Fire / Earth / Water / Metal”

This is about **how stress *looks* when it hits you**, not what causes it.

Think behavior, not philosophy.

**Air stress**

* mind racing
* overthinking everything
* talking or texting too much
* jumping between ideas
* can’t sit still mentally

**Fire stress**

* anger or impatience
* reacting fast
* wanting to confront someone
* bursts of energy
* impulsive decisions

**Earth stress**

* shutting down
* feeling heavy or stuck
* avoiding things
* procrastinating
* comfort eating or sleeping

**Water stress**

* emotional waves
* feeling overwhelmed
* absorbing other people’s feelings
* withdrawing
* needing solitude

**Metal stress**

* perfectionism spikes
* harsh self-criticism
* becoming extremely disciplined or rigid
* trying to control everything

You pick the one that **looks most like you when things go wrong**.

## 2. “Big decisions work best when I…”

This reveals **your decision style**.

Here’s the difference.

### Quiet instinct

You feel a subtle inner signal.
Not loud. Just a calm internal “yes” or “no.”

People with this style say things like:

> “I just knew.”

---

### Calm logic

You need to **think it through clearly**.

You want:

* facts
* pros and cons
* reasoning

You trust your **mind**.

---

### Willpower

You decide quickly and **commit**.

You’re comfortable saying:

> “I’m doing this.”

Even without perfect information.

---

### Sleep on it

Your mind works **while resting**.

You often wake up knowing the answer.

These people say:

> “Let me think about it tonight.”

---

### Gut yes/no

Your body reacts instantly.

It’s visceral.

Your stomach or chest tells you **yes or no immediately**.
