You are working on an existing app called:

Soul Codex

Your job is NOT to rebuild the app.
Your job is to replace the current onboarding flow with a better one that:
- gets stronger signal
- feels less annoying
- avoids repetitive personality-test language
- reduces cloned profiles
- gives users value faster

==================================================
PRIMARY GOAL
==================================================

Redesign onboarding so it collects:
1. core birth data
2. a few high-signal behavioral refinement answers
3. then immediately guides the user into value:
   - Profile
   - Today
   - Codex

Do NOT turn onboarding into a long quiz.
Do NOT ask broad identity questions that force people into fake fixed labels.

==================================================
NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES
==================================================

1. Do NOT rebuild the whole app.
2. Do NOT change public output contracts unless absolutely necessary.
3. Do NOT redesign backend/core architecture in this pass.
4. Do NOT add lots of extra questions.
5. Do NOT repeat questions the user already answered.
6. Do NOT use vague personality-test wording like:
   - "How do you perform under pressure?"
   - "What is your decision style?"
   - "Are you adaptable?"
7. Keep the flow premium, short, and behavior-based.
8. Preserve the current polished visual language where possible.

==================================================
NEW ONBOARDING STRUCTURE
==================================================

Replace or adapt the current onboarding into these steps:

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — CORE BLUEPRINT
--------------------------------------------------

Title:
Let’s build your core blueprint

Fields:
- First name
- Birth date
- Birth time
- Birth place

Helper copy:
Birth date gives your core structure.
Birth time sharpens accuracy.
Birth place helps place the chart correctly.

Additional note:
Don’t know your exact time? Start anyway. Some parts will be less precise.

Goal:
Make this feel premium, calm, and trustworthy.

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — PRESSURE: FIRST REACTION
--------------------------------------------------

Title:
When pressure hits, what happens first?

Instruction:
Pick up to 2.

Options:
- My mind speeds up and I overanalyze
- I go quiet and pull inward
- I handle the problem first and feel it later
- I get sharp or impatient fast
- I change strategy and look for another angle
- It depends who’s involved and whether I feel safe

Rules:
- allow up to 2 selections
- store as primary + secondary pressure pattern
- do not phrase this as permanent identity

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — PRESSURE: WHAT HAPPENS NEXT
--------------------------------------------------

Title:
If the pressure keeps building, what usually happens next?

Instruction:
Pick 1.

Options:
- I get colder and harder to reach
- I detach and stop showing much
- I get exhausted and shut down
- I try to control more than I should
- I get emotionally sharper than I mean to
- I stop trusting myself and hesitate

Goal:
Capture escalation pattern, not just surface reaction.

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 4 — DECISION FRICTION
--------------------------------------------------

Title:
When a big decision matters, what usually gets in the way first?

Instruction:
Pick up to 2.

Options:
- I keep gathering information and delay the move
- I wait for certainty that never fully comes
- I know what I feel, but struggle to trust it
- I decide quickly and rethink it later
- I adapt too much to the pressure around me
- I delay because the stakes feel too permanent

Rules:
- allow up to 2
- store as primary + secondary decision friction

--------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — WHAT DRAINS YOU FASTEST
--------------------------------------------------

Title:
What drains you faster than it should?

Instruction:
Pick up to 2.

Options:
- Too much noise or stimulation
- People who expect instant access to me
- Vague promises and sloppy follow-through
- Making decisions before I feel ready
- Feeling responsible for everyone’s mood
- Being pushed into someone else’s pace

Rules:
- allow up to 2
- store as primary + secondary drain pattern
